## [0.11.1] — 2026-04-25 · Lineage tab is a first-class entry point

The Lineage tab used to be a passive placeholder — it told you to leave and click an entity in the Measures or Columns tab to populate it. Now it's its own first-class entry point with a search-driven workflow.

### Added

- **Lineage tab search bar** — a permanent search input at the top of the Lineage panel. Type any measure or column name; a typeahead dropdown surfaces up to 12 ranked matches with kind chips (`ƒ measure` / `▦ column`), home-table context, and status badges (UNUSED / INDIRECT) where relevant. Click a result to render that entity's lineage immediately.

- **Auto-focus on tab entry.** Switching to the Lineage tab now focuses the search input — single keystroke from "I want to trace X" to typing X.

- **External-click sync.** Clicking a measure / column name elsewhere (Measures, Columns, Pages, Tables, Calc Groups) still fires `navigateLineage()` as before, but the Lineage search box now also picks up that name. Reader can see at a glance "this is the entity I'm looking at" without scrolling up.

- **Smarter empty state.** When the Lineage tab is opened cold, the content area shows a softer prompt: "Type above to search any measure or column, or click an entity in Measures · Columns · Pages · Tables to trace it." — both entry paths surfaced equally.

### Changed

- **Outside-click dismissal** for the typeahead dropdown — clicking anywhere outside the search row hides the suggestions so they don't obscure the lineage canvas.

- **Ranking** — exact match first, then prefix match, then substring. Stable tiebreaker prefers measures over columns (typical search target). Refine-to-narrow hint when more than 12 hits.

### Why

The previous Lineage tab was redundant until activated — readers had to learn the "click elsewhere to populate" idiom before getting any value. The search-driven entry makes the tab self-sufficient: open it, type, get an answer. Click-from-elsewhere remains a first-class flow (it's the natural follow-up after browsing in Measures / Columns), and now the search bar reflects what's being viewed for clarity.
