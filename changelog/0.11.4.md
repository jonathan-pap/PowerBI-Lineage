## [0.11.4] — 2026-04-26 · Visitor count on landing overlay

Public, anonymous page-view tally on the landing card. Surfaces real-world reach without leaking anything about visitors.

### Added
- **GoatCounter visit badge** — 4th chip on the landing trust line shows the live page-view count. The badge is a link to the public stats page (`https://jpapworth.goatcounter.com`), and the chip carries a `title=` tooltip making the data policy explicit: *"Anonymous page-view tally — no cookies, no IP storage"*.
- **GoatCounter tracker `<script>`** baked into the browser shell only, before `</body>`. Loaded with `async` so it never blocks first paint. CLI mode is untouched — no public URL means nothing to track.

### Notes
- GoatCounter is privacy-respecting by design: no cookies, no JS fingerprinting, IPs are hashed with a daily-rotating salt and discarded. The publisher (Jonathan Papworth) sees aggregate page views, referrers, and broad geography only.
- Placement choice: the trust line, not a CTA. The badge sits as a quiet credibility signal next to "267 tests" and "Zero runtime deps" — not as a vanity metric. Visitors who care can click through; everyone else doesn't notice.
- Why a 4th chip vs a separate row: keeps the landing card's visual gravity on the two CTAs ("Open folder" / "Try a sample"). A standalone counter row would compete for attention.
